Personal Info

I'm not sure exactly where to start, as it is always difficult to talk about yourself. But, I spose I will tell you a little about myself. I'm a fun outgoing person that loves to have a good laugh. I enjoy doing anything that involves being around a lot of people. I also have a love/hate relationship with Iowa State Athletics.

Some of my vices include binge watching shows on Netflix or Amazon, and have seen most seasons of pretty much every popular TV show. I also like to go to the Movie Theatre to watch the newest movies. When I had more time, I also really enjoyed playing video games. (Some of which I played way too much of at certain points of my life). I still play a little every now and then when I find the time.

I spent a majority of my life working in restaurants and have basically done every job inside of a restaurant, from cooking to bartending to management. As a result, (although it doesn't really show) I love food. I really enjoy dining out at restaurants, and even try to pick one that I've never been to before on occasion just to try it out.

More recently I worked as an IT recruiter. I really enjoyed the people I met and the people I worked with. In the end I realized that although meeting new people and the work was great, I had a passion to be on the development side instead of being the guy that recruited the developers. I had done a tiny bit of Web Development in high school and decided that I really did have a passion for it. I think the main reason I really enjoy it is that I really like to learn new things. When I was recruiting I learned that it was a field that was ever changing, and in a lot of cases pretty rapidly. Giving me an opportunity to be continously learning. I also like the idea of being able to create something for a lot of people to enjoy and make their lives easier.

Education

I graduated from Iowa State with a Marketing Degree. Being in sales was something that I had always thought I wanted to do. I think the main reason was because I really enjoy meeting new people, learning their story, and building those relationships. I also was always a talker, so it was something I felt really fit. Though, when I was actually in a sales position, I quickly learned that making a bunch of phone calls a day wasn't something for me. Meeting new people was great, but for the most part the day to day was really mundane for me and didn't seem to provide the challenges I craved.

After some deep soul searching in December of 2016, I decided it was time to listen to my heart and go back to school for Web Development at DMACC. So I quit my job as an IT recruiter and enrolled in classes for the Spring Semester of 2017. So far classes have been great, and I have really enjoyed it. Come December 2018 I will have completed all of my courses.

I currently have a working knowledge of HTML/CSS, Bootsrap, SASS, WordPress, Photoshop, and I would say a fairly decent understanding of vanilla JavaScript. I look forward to learning more JavaScript as it is what I have enjoyed the most.
